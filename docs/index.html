🚨 Real Incident — Life Healthcare June 2020

In June 2020, Life Healthcare Group — operating 66 hospitals across 7 South African provinces and Botswana — was hit by a targeted criminal ransomware attack during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic. Admissions, billing, email and financial systems were taken offline for 28 days. Patient care was maintained through manual fallback procedures. The company did NOT pay the ransom and restored from clean backups.

💡 7 Key Lessons from the Real Incident
1

Do not pay the ransom — Life Healthcare restored from backups without paying. Paying funds criminal activity and does not guarantee decryption.

2

Manual fallback procedures saved patient care — 66 hospitals operated on paper for 28 days without a single patient being harmed.

3

Your RTO targets are probably wrong — A 4-hour RTO target became 28 days of actual downtime. Test your recovery before a crisis forces the test.

4

Restore from before the compromise date — Ransomware can be dormant for weeks. A recent backup may contain the attacker's foothold.

5

Engage external forensics immediately — Do not attempt internal investigation. Preserve evidence. Life Healthcare brought in external teams on Day 1.

6

POPIA Section 22 is triggered early — Notify the Information Regulator on reasonable grounds — not only after confirmation.

7

Pre-approved crisis communications are essential — Life Healthcare's measured, consistent messaging protected both patients and reputation.
