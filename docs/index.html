// KEY LESSONS

7 Lessons from the Real Incident

1
Do not pay the ransom — Life Healthcare restored from backups without paying. Paying funds criminal activity and does not guarantee decryption.
2
Manual fallback procedures saved patient care — 66 hospitals operated on paper for 28 days without a single patient being harmed.
3
Your RTO targets are probably wrong — A 4-hour RTO became 28 days of actual downtime. Test your recovery before a crisis forces the test.
4
Restore from before the compromise date — Ransomware can be dormant for weeks. A recent backup may contain the attacker's foothold.
5
Engage external forensics immediately — Do not attempt internal investigation. Preserve evidence. Life Healthcare brought in external teams on Day 1.
6
POPIA Section 22 is triggered early — Notify the Information Regulator on reasonable grounds — not only after confirmation.
7
Pre-approved crisis communications are essential — Life Healthcare's measured, consistent messaging protected both patients and reputation throughout.
